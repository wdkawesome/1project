About Hamish

Hamish MaDonald has been an SNP MSP since June 2010 representing the constituency of the Western Isles. Hamish gained a PhD in Scots Language from Newton Stewart University in 1995 before joining the Western Isles Telegraph as Lead Agriculture and Wildlife Reporter. Outside of politics Hamish is a keen cyclist and an avid Loch Ness Monster spotter. When Hamish isn't competing in cycle sportives or camping on the banks of Loch Ness, he can be found practicing his boomerang carpentary skills in his garden shed. Hamish is also the only known person to be able to fluently speak the Scots Language backwards after suffering a strike to the head from a returning self made boomerang.

Hamish takes great pride in his charity work and was the key founder of BAS (Boomerang Awareness Scotiety) which aims to educate the general public on the dangers of boomerangs. He has also taken part in several charity bike rides and disputes claims that he used charity money to buy his most recent carbon fibre time trial bike.
